Work

My research is in the broad area of geospatial big data analytics. I bring a geographic perspective and an expertise in GIS and spatial data science to bear on interesting interdisciplinary questions of sustainability, the spatial representation of socioeconomic activity, human mobility, and the utilization of artificial intelligence to facilitate our decision-making process.

Sustainability Science

one of my ongoing lines of research is to measure and assess sustainable development through remote sensing and GIS. I am currently collaborating with the United Nations Research Institute for Social Development to develop low-cost, scalable, and context-based sustainable development performance indicators for water consumption and carbon emission to support the Sustainable Development Goals. My research interest focuses on incorporating statistical and intelligent algorithms to automatically process remote sensing data (e.g., the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite nighttime lights) and utilizing cloud computing and environment to address socioeconomic development and big geospatial data challenges specifically for less developed regions around the world.

Human Mobility

With the development of location-based sensing techniques, human mobility datasets collected from different sensors have increased significantly. Performing spatiotemporal queries on human mobility data becomes crucial in supporting various types of advanced analysis and decision-making. My research interest along this line focuses on (1) human mobility analysis and its applications in transportation planning and (2) predicting and mitigating the impacts of unexpected events (e.g., extreme weather and public health emergency) on human activities.

GeoAI, Geovisualization, and HPC

As more organizations across an expanding range of domains are recognizing the importance of spatial data analysis for more diverse purposes, the visual-based analysis of complex and vast data becomes a vital part of an analytics infrastructure. There is a need to provide scalable interactive visualization solutions in the cloud for ultra-scale multidimensional datasets, so they are accessible for scientists and researchers to perform advanced data analysis for multiple purposes. Moreover, it is crucial to transform the relevant geographical and environmental multidimensional data into meaningful information so that they can be effectively utilized by the public, workers, planners, and policymakers.
